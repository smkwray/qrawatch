Project Overview

This project measures Treasury maturity composition using public data. It tracks coupon shortfall relative to a bill-share baseline, studies announcement effects around Quarterly Refunding Announcements, traces transmission through the bills / reserves / ON RRP channel, and nests Treasury issuance inside a broader public-duration-supply framework. All data is sourced from public APIs and filings: FiscalData, FRED, Treasury QRA documents, and SEC EDGAR.

QRA Event Study

Market reactions around Quarterly Refunding Announcements, classified by expected policy direction. Easing events (coupon restraint) should lower yields and term premia; tightening events (more duration supply) should raise them. The current event sample is still small, so these results should be read as suggestive rather than definitive.

Plumbing: Bills, Reserves & ON RRP

Weekly regressions testing whether bill-heavy financing is associated with larger declines in ON RRP and smaller declines in reserve balances, consistent with a substitution channel where bills absorb money-market liquidity that would otherwise sit in the Fed’s facilities.

Public Duration Supply

A joint measure of Treasury non-bill issuance, Fed QT, and buybacks. Higher values indicate more duration supply to the public. The headline measure uses exact net issuance; the provisional construction uses gross coupon flows as a fallback.

Pricing: Rates & Term Premium

Reduced-form pricing regressions linking release-level Maturity-Tilt Flow, monthly carry-forward context specs, Excess Bills Stock, and Public Duration Supply to the 10-year yield and 10-year term premium proxy. These outputs remain supporting/provisional in interpretation, but the release-level flow design is now the project’s main credibility test for neutral maturity-to-rates estimates.

QRA Watch

An open research project studying whether Treasury maturity composition choices have balance-sheet-policy-like effects on market prices and liquidity conditions. The project does not claim activist intent. It measures observable issuance composition and related market outcomes using public data.

Public data sources: FiscalData, FRED, Treasury QRA documents, and SEC EDGAR.
