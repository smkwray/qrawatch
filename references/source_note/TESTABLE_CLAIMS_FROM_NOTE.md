# Testable Claims Distilled from the Source Note

This memo converts the note’s arguments into researchable claims without requiring the paper to adopt the note’s rhetoric.

## 1. Missing-coupons measurement claim

The key measurable object is not gross issuance or WAM by itself. It is the quantity of **net coupon issuance avoided** by funding more through bills than a normal bill-share range would imply.

Empirical translation:

- build ATI / missing-coupons series from quarter financing needs and net bill issuance
- test sensitivity to 15%, 18%, and 20% targets
- compare with WAM only as a robustness check

## 2. QRA announcement claim

Markets react when the Treasury communicates a change in expected issuance composition through the quarterly refunding process.

Empirical translation:

- use QRA dates as event-study timing
- compare official date and T-1 date
- test term premium, long yields, and slope reactions

## 3. Plumbing mechanism claim

Bills matter not just because they are shorter duration, but because they are closer substitutes for cash / reserves / ON RRP placements than coupons are.

Empirical translation:

- test whether larger bill issuance is associated with larger ON RRP drawdown
- test whether bills imply smaller reserve drain than equivalent coupon issuance
- include TGA and QT controls

## 4. Combined-policy claim

Treasury issuance and Fed QT should be read jointly because both affect the amount of duration the public must absorb.

Empirical translation:

- create a combined public-duration-supply measure
- compare Treasury-only and Treasury+Fed specifications

## 5. Flow-versus-stock claim

The note raises a flow-versus-stock issue: whether prices respond more to the current financing mix or to the cumulative stock composition of debt.

Empirical translation:

- run horse races between current ATI flow and stock excess-bill measures
- use event studies for short windows and lower-frequency regressions for persistence

## 6. Buyback extension claim

Treasury buybacks can work as a mini duration-management tool and should be folded into the broader duration-supply framework.

Empirical translation:

- subtract buyback accepted amount from public duration supply
- later test buyback event dates directly

## 7. What not to assume

The note contains intent-related rhetoric. The core paper does not need that to succeed.

A stronger empirical paper asks:

- what changed,
- when it was communicated,
- what prices moved,
- and what plumbing channel can explain the effect.
