# Project Brief

## Working title

**Treasury maturity composition as shadow balance-sheet policy: measurement, announcement effects, and transmission**

## One-sentence version

Study whether shifts in Treasury financing from coupons toward bills behave like a shadow balance-sheet policy by lowering public duration supply, changing term premia, and easing market plumbing.

## Why this is the strongest consolidated project

The note’s core claims naturally collapse into one empirical chain:

1. Treasury deviates from a bill-share norm, creating “missing coupons.”
2. Those deviations are communicated through the QRA process.
3. Lower coupon supply reduces public duration supply and affects term premia.
4. Bill-heavy financing works partly through the bills / reserves / ON RRP substitution channel.
5. Fed QT and Treasury issuance should be analyzed jointly rather than as separate policy domains.

That gives one coherent project with:

- a measurement object,
- an announcement-based identification design,
- a transmission mechanism,
- and a macro-financial interpretation.

## Neutral framing

The paper does **not** need to prove activist intent.

A stronger academic framing is:

> Regardless of motive, do Treasury maturity-composition choices have balance-sheet-policy-like effects on market prices and liquidity conditions?

This moves the project away from contested political interpretation and toward observable public-data evidence.

## Main question

**Do shifts in the Treasury bill-versus-coupon mix operate like a shadow form of balance-sheet policy by reducing public duration supply, lowering term premia, and easing financial conditions?**

## Core hypotheses

### H1. Measurement

A quarter-level ATI series based on **missing coupons** captures economically meaningful deviations from a regular bill-share range better than WAM alone.

### H2. Announcement effects

QRA announcements that imply more coupon supply or faster term-out raise term premia and long yields relative to announcements that imply coupon restraint.

### H3. Plumbing channel

Bill-heavy financing is associated with larger declines in ON RRP and smaller declines in reserve balances than equivalent duration-bearing issuance.

### H4. Combined policy stance

A joint measure of **Treasury duration supply + Fed runoff - Treasury buybacks** explains term-premium variation better than looking at Treasury or the Fed in isolation.

### H5. Flow versus stock

Flow-based measures of new duration supply outperform stock-only measures in short-window pricing exercises, while stock measures may matter more for medium-horizon persistence.

## Main contribution

The contribution is not simply a replication of the note. It is a reframing:

- translate controversial narrative into neutral, testable claims,
- build the measure using free public data,
- identify effects around QRA dates,
- trace the plumbing channel through ON RRP / reserves / TGA,
- and nest Treasury issuance inside a broader public-duration-supply framework.

## What belongs in the main paper

Keep these at the center:

- ATI / missing-coupons measurement
- QRA announcement event studies
- bills / reserves / ON RRP plumbing tests
- combined Treasury + Fed duration-supply measure
- flow-versus-stock comparison

## What should stay secondary

Do **not** lead with these:

- election-cycle intent
- partisan motive
- broad legal claims
- debt-management norm violations as the only issue
- cost-versus-rollover-risk as the main story

Those are interesting side sections or extensions, but they are weaker as the paper’s organizing principle.

## First-round deliverables

A strong first draft only needs:

1. a clean ATI seed series
2. a four-event QRA table
3. a weekly plumbing prototype
4. a provisional combined-duration-supply series
5. robustness on target bill share and event-date definition

## Success criterion

If the repo can produce:

- a quarter-level ATI table,
- a QRA event-study summary,
- and at least one weekly plumbing regression with sensible signs,

then the project has crossed from concept into a real empirical paper.
